Intelligence Brief Vyvgart Hytrulo · CIDP June 5, 2026 · 6:42 AM ET

The IVIg transition question is now the defining clinical conversation in CIDP. And self-injection is changing what patients expect from treatment.

Five signals are moving this week. The IVIg-to-efgartigimod transition carries real relapse risk — and a Phase 4 trial is now testing a safer switch without requiring disease worsening first. The prefilled syringe approval has released a patient narrative about autonomy that is growing fast. Payers are beginning to model budget impact at scale. And argenx remains the only FcRn blocker with a CIDP indication — a window that will not stay open indefinitely.

IndicationCIDP · gMG (AChR+)

● Situation Room  ·  5 Active Signals

The strategic picture this week: argenx is the sole FcRn blocker approved for CIDP — a first-mover position generating both clinical enthusiasm and real-world friction. The IVIg transition narrative is bifurcating: academic neurologists are cautiously optimistic while community practice is seeing relapse events that are making prescribers hesitate. The prefilled syringe has opened a patient discourse around home self-injection that argenx has the right to own — but hasn't yet fully claimed. Payer concern over budget impact is surfacing in P&T committee language. Rystiggo's SC approval in gMG is a signal the CIDP competitive window is narrowing.

01Providers · Clinical Practice

The IVIg-to-Vyvgart transition carries real relapse risk. A Phase 4 trial is now testing a switch without requiring disease worsening.

↑ AcceleratingClinical Practice Signal

A case series published in Pharmacy Times documented four patients who experienced severe CIDP relapse after switching from stable IVIg to efgartigimod. The ADHERE trial design required patients to worsen before starting — creating a real clinical hazard when replicated in practice. A Phase 4 trial led by Yessar Hussain, MD (UT Austin Dell Medical School), presented at PNS Annual Meeting 2025, tests whether patients can transition one week after their last IVIg infusion, without requiring disease worsening first.

"The ADHERE trial required patients to worsen before starting efgartigimod. In real practice, deliberately allowing a patient to relapse before initiating therapy is not always feasible or ethical. We need a better transition framework."

— Yessar Hussain, MD · PNS Annual Meeting, May 2025 · NeurologyLive, May 2026

02Patients · SC Administration

The prefilled syringe has unlocked a new patient narrative: "I do my injection in the morning and go to work."

● EmergingSC · Self-Injection

The 20-to-30-second injection at home is being described in stark contrast to the IVIg infusion center experience. This narrative is patient-originated, not brand-originated.

03Payers · Reimbursement

A 35% budget impact model is now in payer channels. Prior authorization for CIDP is being drafted for the first time.

↑ AcceleratingBudget Impact Signal

A budget impact model presented at AANEM 2025 calculated a 35.5% projected increase in healthcare spending for a 25-million-member plan assuming 10% efgartigimod uptake in CIDP year one. Both modeled scenarios produced near-identical results. This model is now circulating in payer channels. Prior authorization requirements for CIDP that did not exist 12 months ago are being drafted now.

"A 35% budget impact is significant enough that payers will respond with utilization management. The question is whether the clinical benefit story is strong enough to push back against step therapy requirements."

— Betsy Lahue MPH, Alkemi · AANEM 2025 · NeurologyLive, Feb 2026

04Competitive Landscape

Rystiggo SC approved in gMG. argenx holds the CIDP-only position — the competitive window is 12–18 months.

▲ Competitor MoveCompetitive Monitor

UCB's Rystiggo (rozanolixizumab) received SC approval in gMG in May 2026 — the first head-to-head competitor with a subcutaneous formulation. As of June 2026, Vyvgart Hytrulo remains the only FcRn blocker approved for CIDP; Rystiggo's CIDP trial is ongoing. Nipocalimab (IMAAVY, J&J) is approved for gMG (AChR+/MuSK+) but not CIDP. A J&J indirect comparison claimed superior gMG-ADL responses at week 8 for nipocalimab vs efgartigimod — data being used in gMG field conversations but not applicable to CIDP.

05Post-AAN 2026 Signal

87.5% early benefit in treatment-naïve CIDP. The earlier-line narrative is forming — field medical needs to reinforce it now.

↑ ActivePost-Congress

At AAN Chicago (April 18–22, 2026), argenx presented an ADHERE post-hoc analysis showing 87.5% of treatment-naïve CIDP patients achieved early benefit from Vyvgart Hytrulo. A Medscape article published April 23, 2026 — written for neurologists, independent of argenx — cited efgartigimod as "offering additional options, particularly for patients who cannot tolerate immunoglobulin." The earlier-line narrative is forming in clinical media. Every week this framing isn't reinforced in the field is a week the default stays with IVIg.

Bottom Line  ·  4 Things That Matter This Week
01

The IVIg transition relapse signal is the most consequential clinical conversation in CIDP right now. Field medical needs a safe-switch framework and must know the Hussain Phase 4 trial exists — before a prescriber's personal relapse experience becomes Vyvgart's defining narrative in their practice.

02

The self-injection narrative is patient-originated and growing. Prescribers need to know about the My VYVGART Path Nurse Case Manager home-training program — this differentiator is not being communicated at scale.

03

Rystiggo SC is now approved in gMG. The competitive window for argenx's sole CIDP position is 12–18 months. The CIDP differentiation story needs to be sharpened in MSL conversations now, not after Rystiggo's CIDP readout.

04

The ADHERE post-hoc data (87.5% early benefit in treatment-naïve patients) is the most important piece of evidence for moving from "second-line after IVIg failure" to "consider earlier." Every day that framing isn't in the field is a day the default stays with IVIg.
